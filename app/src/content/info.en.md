## No Account Required

No sign-up, no password, no phone number. When you open few.chat for the first time, a private identity is created directly on your device.

few.chat does not maintain a central user account database or a public list of its users.

## Your Messages Are Private

Every message is end-to-end encrypted. Only you and the person you are messaging can read it — no one else, not even few.chat.

## You Control Your Contacts

There is no central contact search. You decide who you connect with and share your connection information with that person outside any intermediary service controlled by few.chat.

few.chat does not select recipients, establish contacts, or add anyone to an existing connection.

## Groups

Group conversations are also end-to-end encrypted.

few.chat does not operate server-side group rooms and cannot add or remove members or technically reconstruct a group.

## Your Communication Belongs to You

few.chat has no technical ability to:

* add participants to a conversation,
* block individual connections or contacts,
* read or moderate messages,
* recover lost keys,
* reconstruct encrypted sessions after the fact, or
* access a user’s conversations on their behalf.

If a local identity or key is lost, few.chat cannot recover it.

## Your Data Stays on Your Device

Your identity, name, avatar, contacts, and keys are managed locally on your device.

Your profile is not published. It is shared only by you with the people you choose to share it with.

few.chat does not create a central address book or a server-side communication profile of its users.

## Free and Without Tracking

few.chat is free. There are no ads, no tracking for advertising purposes, and no cookies.

few.chat does not sell user data or create profiles of communication relationships.

## Open and Interchangeable Infrastructure

few.chat is based on open protocols and is not tied to a proprietary communication platform.

The application uses Nostr to exchange encrypted messages. Direct messages are encrypted using NIP-17 Gift Wrap. This protects both the message content and additional communication metadata from the relays being used.

Relays are used solely for the technical transport of encrypted data. They do not maintain few.chat user accounts, manage contacts, or hold keys capable of decrypting messages. Users can generally use other compatible relays or operate their own infrastructure.

Group conversations use MLS, an open standard for end-to-end encrypted group communication. Group membership and the associated keys are managed by the participating devices, not by a central few.chat server.

Because the protocols used are openly documented, their operation can be independently reviewed and implemented in other compatible software.

## Software Instead of a Central Chat Platform

few.chat provides the software that allows users to communicate directly with one another. There is no central intermediary that assigns contacts, suggests conversation partners, or manages conversations.

In particular, few.chat does not operate:

* a public user directory,
* a search or matchmaking function,
* centrally managed contact lists,
* server-side chat rooms, or
* central group management.

Connections are created solely at the initiative of the users involved, for example by directly exchanging a personal link or other connection information.
